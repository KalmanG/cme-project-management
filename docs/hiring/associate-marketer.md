# Associate Marketer

**Department:** Marketing
**Reports to:** Head of Marketing / Marketing Manager
**Level:** Associate (mid-level)
**Work arrangement:** Remote (US or EU hours) — open to hybrid in Boston, MA
**Compensation range:** $55,000 – $75,000 USD base, commensurate with experience; eligible for performance bonus and equity participation
**Status:** Full-time

---

## About CME Wizard

CME Wizard is the easiest way for clinicians to find, compare, and complete the continuing medical education they actually need. We're a small, fast-moving team working to make CME transparent, searchable, and useful — and we're hiring an Associate Marketer to help us grow our audience, partners, and brand.

## The role

You'll own day-to-day execution across content, campaigns, and analytics. You'll work directly with the founders and the broader team to turn strategy into shipped work — landing pages, email sequences, social posts, partner outreach, and the measurement that tells us what's working. This is a generalist role with room to specialize in whichever of those muscles you grow fastest.

## What you'll do

- **Content:** Draft and edit blog posts, landing pages, email sequences, and social copy aimed at clinicians, hospitals, and CME providers. Work with subject-matter experts to translate dense source material into clear, useful writing.
- **Campaigns:** Plan and run multi-channel campaigns (email, paid search, paid social, partner co-marketing). Coordinate creative, copy, and launch timing. Brief external contractors (designers, PPC specialists) as needed.
- **Analytics & reporting:** Set up and maintain dashboards in GA4, Looker, or similar. Pull weekly and monthly performance reports. Surface what's working, what isn't, and what to try next.
- **SEO:** Support keyword research, on-page optimization, internal linking, and content QA. Partner with the product team on structured data and technical SEO.
- **Lifecycle & CRM:** Build and send newsletters and lifecycle emails. Maintain segments and suppression lists. Work with the team on nurture and re-engagement flows.
- **Partner & community outreach:** Research prospective CME providers, specialty societies, and clinician communities. Draft outreach and co-marketing proposals.
- **Coordination:** Keep the marketing calendar and issue tracker up to date. Coordinate reviews, approvals, and launches across internal and external stakeholders.
- **Brand:** Help maintain voice, style, and visual consistency across every surface.

## Success in the first 6 months

- Ship a consistent publishing cadence (blog + newsletter + social) with measurable audience growth.
- Launch at least two full campaigns end-to-end (planning → creative → send → report).
- Stand up a single source of truth for marketing KPIs that the team actually uses.
- Build a shortlist of 20+ qualified partner prospects with warm outreach sent.

## What we're looking for

### Required

- 1–3 years of experience in marketing, communications, growth, or a closely related field (agency, in-house, nonprofit, or political campaign experience all count).
- Strong written communication — you can draft clear, accurate copy without a lot of handholding and edit your own work.
- Analytical comfort — you're at home in spreadsheets and reporting tools, and you can tell a real trend from noise.
- Organized and dependable — you can own a calendar, hit deadlines, and keep stakeholders informed without being prompted.
- Comfortable working async with a small, distributed team.

### Preferred (nice to have, not required)

- Healthcare, life sciences, or clinician-audience marketing experience.
- Hands-on experience with paid search, paid social, or display advertising (Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn).
- SEO fundamentals and working knowledge of GA4.
- CRO / landing-page testing experience.
- WordPress administration, HTML/CSS basics, or light graphic design (Canva, Figma, Photoshop).
- B2B lead generation or sales enablement experience.
- Familiarity with CME, medical education, or adjacent regulated industries.

## How we work

- Small team, tight feedback loops, async-first with a weekly sync.
- Flexible hours; we care about output, not chair time.
- You'll have real ownership of campaigns and measurable goals from day one.
- We're committed to building an inclusive team. We encourage candidates from underrepresented backgrounds to apply, and we review JDs for biased or exclusionary language before every posting.
